# Natural Cycles -- User Reviews

Research collected Apr 2026. Verbatim quotes only. Sorted into Loves / Hates / Wishes.

Sources: App Store (Apple + Google Play), Trustpilot (3.8/5, 84+ pages of reviews), peer-reviewed mixed methods study of NC app store reviews (PMC10685276, published Nov 2023, analyzed thousands of Apple App Store reviews), The Lowdown contraceptives database, Reddit communities (r/FAMnNFP, r/birthcontrol, r/TTC), trackyourfertility.com critique, Natural Cycles customer help pages.

Lanae's relevance is flagged where the review touches: chronic illness, POTS, endometriosis, irregular cycles, hormone sensitivity, or the specific clinical scenarios in her data.

## Hates (20 quotes)

### Algorithm is not built for irregular cycles / chronic illness

31. "Honestly, this app is only useful if you are a very specific person with a very specific set of circumstances. But for me? After two years of trying, I gave up. That isn't to say it doesn't work. For a very specific type of woman, it certainly does. But not all women have the same lives, interests, or goals. This app has literally no benefit for those of us who fall outside the 'norm.'" (PMC10685276)
   *Lanae relevance: CORE CRITICISM. Lanae is "outside the norm." Her cycles are irregular, her POTS adds noise, her sleep schedule varies. This quote is the warning sign for us.*

32. "My cycle is extremely regular but is longer than usual (38 days) and the app is slightly too slow at realising that this is indeed how I function. I'm going onto the 3rd month using it and it still keeps predicting my ovulation and period on the wrong days." (PMC10685276)
   *Lanae relevance: our implementation must not require 3 cycles of convergence before giving any value.*

33. "I'm always in pain when ovulating and I have double checked it with an LH test but when I log it on the app, it shows my ovulation was on another day. How can I change it so it will reflect my pain?" (PMC10685276)
   *Lanae relevance: user's subjective ovulation signal (ovulation pain, mittelschmerz) overrides algorithm for them. Lanae reports ovulation pain. Our version should let her self-mark ovulation if she is confident.*

34. "Great if you're on normal cycles. Not particularly helpful post-birth and breastfeeding." (PMC10685276)

35. "If your cycle is very irregular, Natural Cycles may be less suitable for you." (NC's own customer support acknowledgment)

36. "It can take up to several cycles for the app to get to know a woman's system and accurately predict fertility." (NC help article, summarized in third-party reviews)

### Algorithm conservatism (too many red days)

37. "It took a while to get a good portion of green days vs red" (Trustpilot, 4 stars)

38. "The algorithm errs on the side of caution when it comes to Red Days so it did take a couple months for users to start seeing more Green Days." (Third-party summary of NC behavior)
   *Lanae relevance: conservatism is safe for contraception but may obscure the cycle phase for women using NC just for tracking. Our "phase view" should never hide phase info behind a safety buffer.*

### Retroactive day re-coloring (trust breach)

39. "the app changing green days back to red, getting green days and then the next day saying the green day was actually red." (Apple App Store review trend documented in multiple aggregators)
   *Lanae relevance: if we retroactively recompute cycle phase when new data arrives, we need to clearly mark what changed and why. Silent re-coloring destroys trust.*

### Missing cervical mucus signal

40. "Natural Cycles assigns green (safe) days very early in the cycle, often far beyond what any established method would give, and even from your very first cycle." (trackyourfertility.com critique)

41. "Your temperature confirms ovulation only after it's happened. For contraceptive purposes, users need to know before ovulation occurs, making temperature-only tracking insufficient." (trackyourfertility.com critique)
   *Lanae relevance: BBT-only is a known architectural weakness. Our multi-signal approach (BBT + HRV + RHR + Oura + manual mucus) is specifically designed to mitigate this.*

## Key cross-cutting themes for LanaeHealth

1. **The promise**: "I understand my body." This is what NC sells. Our app inherits the same promise through the Patterns page and cycle visualization.

2. **The failure mode**: users outside the "normal" range feel unsupported. Lanae IS outside the normal range. Every feature we build must be tested on her data first.

3. **Trust and transparency**: retroactive re-coloring hurts trust. Multi-signal displays (temp + HRV + Oura sleep temp) must show WHICH signal shifted the prediction.

4. **Partner involvement**: demonstrably well-loved. Clancy is already the effective partner here. A partner-style view or summary is additive value.

5. **Wearable passive collection**: the single largest friction reducer NC has. We inherit this via Oura.

6. **Not a contraceptive**: we must never position our cycle work as birth control. NC has Class II FDA device status. We do not and will not.
