# All-in-One Health Apps -- Competitive Analysis

## Apple Health
**LOVE:** Free, built-in, strong privacy (on-device processing), unified overnight vitals card, Sleep Focus, PDF export
**HATE:** No backend API (data locked on phone), no food tracking, limited insights, cumbersome manual logging, killed Apple Health+ AI coach, requires Apple Watch for most features, no actionable recommendations
**WISH:** Better data insights, food/nutrition integration, cross-device access, actual AI coaching, desktop/web version

## Google Fit / Health Connect
**LOVE:** AI health coach (2026 preview), CGM integration coming, medical records linking, broad ecosystem
**HATE:** Deprecated Google Fit (confusing migration), Health Connect doesn't sync with Samsung Health, one-way syncs only, Strava/Garmin don't integrate, $9.99/mo Premium for insights
**WISH:** True bidirectional sync, Samsung data access, stable standard (not deprecation cycle), Garmin/Strava integration

## Samsung Health
**LOVE:** Deep Samsung device integration, broad metric coverage
**HATE:** Blocked partner apps from writing step data, won't sync to Health Connect, designed for Samsung lock-in
**WISH:** Open data sharing, less proprietary ecosystem

## Why All-in-One Apps Fail
- Apple Health aggregates but cannot analyze
- Google Fit aggregates but deprecated itself
- Samsung Health aggregates but siloes everything
- None have food tracking, symptom tracking, or medical record parsing
- None offer clinical intelligence or doctor-ready reports
- They are infrastructure, not solutions
