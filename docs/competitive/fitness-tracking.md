# Fitness/Workout Tracking -- Competitive Analysis

## Strava (Cardio/GPS King)
**LOVE:** Social feed and community, segment leaderboards, device-agnostic, route discovery, Year in Sport
**HATE:** Subscription creep, sued Garmin (massive backlash), feature bloat, not for strength training, Apple Health sync issues
**WISH:** Better strength integration, disable gamification, privacy controls

## Strong (Strength Logging Gold Standard)
**LOVE:** 3-tap set logging (fastest UX), template system, progressive overload tracking, CSV export, lifetime purchase option ($99)
**HATE:** No AI suggestions, no social, premium-gated features, limited free tier (3 routines)
**WISH:** AI programming, social features, better cardio integration

## Fitbod (AI Workouts)
**LOVE:** AI generates each session, muscle recovery heat map, equipment adaptability, 27% faster 1RM improvement
**HATE:** Wild weight recommendations (1,300 lbs for shrugs), no injury/pain filter, poor retention (drop-off after ~7 workouts), $16/mo
**WISH:** Injury avoidance filter, rest day recommendations, workout plan structure

## Hevy (Rising, Best Free Tier)
**LOVE:** Unlimited workouts free, social network built in, modern clean UI, $24/yr (cheapest serious tracker), HevyGPT
**HATE:** Less advanced analytics than Strong, limited set types, social can distract
**WISH:** Deeper analytics, better Apple Watch app

## FitrWoman / WILD.AI (Cycle-Aware Fitness)
**LOVE:** 5 cycle phases tracked, phase-specific training/nutrition/supplement recommendations, wearable integration, covers full reproductive lifespan
**HATE:** Limited beyond cycle-aware features
**WISH:** Deeper chronic illness support

## Visible (Chronic Illness Pacing)
**LOVE:** Morning Stability Score, PacePoints energy budget, real-time pacing notifications, 86% of users feel more in control
**HATE:** Not a fitness app (it's an illness management app)
**WISH:** Combined with actual exercise tracking

## Our Edge
- Chronic illness exercise mode with safe ceiling (based on PEM history)
- Position-aware for POTS (recumbent/seated/standing, Levine/Dallas protocol)
- Post-exercise symptom tracking (12-48h follow-up notification)
- HR delta from resting (meaningful for POTS vs absolute HR)
- Cycle phase + chronic condition awareness (WILD.AI + Visible combined)
- Recovery intelligence factoring autonomic dysfunction, sleep, cycle
- "Gentle wins" celebration (completing 10-min recumbent bike during POTS flare)
